🫁 Box Breathing (4-4-4-4)
▼

Activates your parasympathetic nervous system to slow your heart rate. Works in minutes and you can do it anywhere.

  • Breathe IN for 4 counts (feel your belly expand, not just your chest)
  • HOLD for 4 counts
  • Breathe OUT slowly for 4 counts
  • HOLD for 4 counts
  • Repeat 4–6 times. Notice your heart rate slow.

👁️ 5-4-3-2-1 Grounding
▼

Brings you back to the present moment by engaging all five senses. Excellent for dissociation, spiralling thoughts, or panic.

  • Name 5 things you can SEE right now
  • Name 4 things you can TOUCH or feel
  • Name 3 things you can HEAR
  • Name 2 things you can SMELL (or two you love)
  • Name 1 thing you can TASTE

Say them out loud if you can. It helps more than you'd think.

🧊 Cold Water Reset
▼

Based on the mammalian diving reflex — cold water on your face rapidly slows your heart rate. Surprisingly fast and effective for acute distress or a very bad moment.

  • Run cold water and splash your face 3–5 times
  • Or hold your wrists under cold running water for 30 seconds
  • Breathe slowly while you do it
  • Notice the physical shift — it's real
🦶 Body Scan
▼

Pulls attention from anxious thoughts into physical sensation. Particularly useful for dissociation or when you're carrying tension you didn't notice.

  • Sit or lie comfortably. Close your eyes if that feels okay.
  • Start at the crown of your head — notice any tension or tingling
  • Slowly move down: forehead, jaw (is it clenched?), neck, shoulders
  • Continue to chest, stomach, arms, hands
  • Down to hips, thighs, calves, feet, toes
  • Wherever you notice tension: breathe into it, let it soften on the exhale
🏡 Safe Place Visualisation
▼

Your brain responds to vividly imagined safety almost the same way it responds to real safety. Build this slowly so it feels real — it becomes a refuge you can return to anytime.

  • Close your eyes. Breathe slowly.
  • Imagine a place where you feel completely safe and calm — real or invented, no rules
  • Notice the colours, the light, the temperature
  • What can you hear? What does it smell like? What's underfoot?
  • Spend 2–5 minutes just being there. Let your body relax.
